Why second-hand machines?

There are many reasons to consider a used machine over a new one. They are an excellent alternative that can perform the same tasks. Used machines can be monitored and inspected in production, under real conditions. They also have a very high price advantage compared to new machines. Even larger companies prefer to invest in used high-quality and well-maintained machines. High-quality machines are very durable.

With a limited budget, companies can only invest in new machines with lower quality. These machines can cause problems such as downtime during production. Such machine manufacturers may not be able to provide adequate after-sales service, or problems that occur may not be covered by their warranty. There may be quality problems with products. All these problems cause costs that can exceed the cost of a second-hand machine.

For these reasons, the purchase of a used, renewed and well-maintained machine can be more advantageous and cost-efficient. However, the purchase of used machines has other advantages, such as:

Advantages of second-hand machines

  • Available immediately. After the first installation, most problems occur and are solved immediately by the first owner
  • Can be viewed and inspected during production in a live factory under real circumstances → real experience
  • Cost-effective → budget can be divided for multiple machines and systems
  • Used machines are easier and cheaper to maintain

Disadvantages of new machines

  • Very long delivery times, as these are produced on demand. After the first start-up, problems can arise which in turn hinder production
  • Only a preview in the catalogue or in an exhibition
  • Initial purchase very expensive, so the budget cannot be divided between several machines
  • Maintenance and service significantly more expensive
